**Population**: The entire group of individuals or items that a study aims to describe. Example: all students enrolled at a university.

**Sample**: A subset of the population selected for measurement. Example: 200 randomly chosen students from that university.

**Mean (μ or x̄)**: The arithmetic average, computed by summing all values and dividing by the count. Example: the average exam score of a class of 30 students.

**Median**: The middle value when data is sorted in ascending order. Robust to outliers — useful when data is skewed, e.g., household income distributions.

**Mode**: The value that appears most frequently in a dataset. A dataset can have multiple modes (bimodal, multimodal).

**Variance (σ²)**: The average of the squared deviations from the mean. Measures how far data points are spread from the center. Higher variance = more spread out.

**Standard Deviation (σ)**: The square root of the variance. Expressed in the same units as the original data, making it more interpretable than variance.

**Normal Distribution**: A symmetric, bell-shaped probability distribution fully described by its mean and standard deviation. Many natural phenomena approximate this shape.

**Empirical Rule (68-95-99.7 Rule)**: For a normal distribution, approximately 68% of values fall within 1σ, 95% within 2σ, and 99.7% within 3σ of the mean.

**Central Limit Theorem (CLT)**: States that the distribution of sample means approaches a normal distribution as sample size increases, regardless of the population's shape. Requires n ≥ 30 in practice.

**Null Hypothesis (H₀)**: The default assumption that there is no effect or no difference. Hypothesis testing attempts to gather evidence against it.

**Alternative Hypothesis (H₁ or Hₐ)**: The claim the researcher wants to support — that an effect or difference exists.

**p-value**: The probability of observing a result at least as extreme as the data, assuming H₀ is true. A small p-value (e.g., < 0.05) suggests evidence against H₀.

**Significance Level (α)**: The threshold for rejecting H₀, typically 0.05. If p-value < α, reject H₀.

**Confidence Interval**: A range of values that likely contains the true population parameter. A 95% CI means: if we repeated the study 100 times, ~95 of the intervals would contain the true value.

**Correlation Coefficient (r)**: A value between −1 and +1 measuring the strength and direction of the linear relationship between two variables. r = 1 is perfect positive correlation.

**Linear Regression**: A method for modelling the relationship between a dependent variable (y) and one or more independent variables (x) using a best-fit line: y = β₀ + β₁x + ε.

**Outlier**: A data point significantly different from the others. Can heavily influence the mean and regression line; always investigate before removing.

**Skewness**: A measure of asymmetry in a distribution. Positive skew = long right tail; negative skew = long left tail.

**Degrees of Freedom (df)**: The number of independent values in a calculation. For a sample variance, df = n − 1.
